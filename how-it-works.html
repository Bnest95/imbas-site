Process

How It Works

Imbas compares open answers against direct prompts to measure what frontier AI systems volunteer, omit, reframe, or reveal only when asked directly.

A. Ask an open question

Start with a question a typical user might ask — broad, natural, without naming the specific mechanism under study.

B. Ask the targeted question

Ask directly about the mechanism, regulation, source, or detail that may have been left out of the open answer.

C. Compare what surfaced

Document what each model volunteered, what it omitted, and what appeared only after direct prompting. Tag observed signal patterns where applicable.

D. Score the Volunteer Gap

Assign a 0–3 score based on the distance between open and direct answers. Higher scores indicate more relevant information left out of the open response.

E. Preserve the case record

Archive prompts, dates, model names, outputs, scoring rationale, and limitations so the observation can be inspected and challenged.

Where this is going

Today: a growing case archive and public case records.

Next: a tool that rides alongside AI and flags the gap in the moment — surfacing what was left out while you read the answer.
