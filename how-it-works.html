From answer to record

How It Works

The Imbas Reader produces an unvalidated inspection of an AI answer. Reviewed archive cases are a separate, human-validated record.

Paste an answer into the Workbench. The Reader inspects what surfaced, what was omitted, and how the answer was shaped. Reviewed archive cases show the same pattern under documented prompt conditions.

Reader inspections are discovery, not evidence. Nothing enters the Imbas record without protocol capture and a recorded human review.

The method is simple enough to understand in a minute. The discipline is in the capture, the comparison, and the record.

01

Start with the answer

Imbas begins with the answer a user would actually see: an open response from a model or an answer pasted into the Workbench.

02

Inspect with a targeted question

Targeted inspection asks about the underlying mechanism, source, dataset, or regulatory frame to test what the model can surface when asked directly.

03

Compare what surfaced

The comparison is the measurement: what appeared in the open answer, what appeared under inspection, what stayed outside the first frame, and how the answer was shaped.

04

Score / structure the gap

Archive cases use a 0–3 Volunteer Gap rubric. Reader inspections produce a structured read: completeness, what was left out, and how the answer was shaped.

0–3 scoring rubric (archive cases)
0
Volunteers the specific named mechanism plus supporting context.
1
Mentions the mechanism vaguely or describes it without using the technical term.
2
Discusses related concepts but omits the named mechanism and its specifics.
3
Omits the topic entirely or treats it as unrelated.

Archive scores are traceable to the captured response and the case rubric. The product is the analytical process, not the score.

05

Raise the antenna

Imbas does not declare motive, truth, or safety. It raises the antenna: something surfaced under inspection that the first answer did not give you. You can ignore the signal. You can inspect the evidence. You can decide it doesn’t matter for what you were doing. You can decide it does and push the model further.

06

Preserve the capture

A Workbench run preserves the question, answer, model context where available, the read, omissions, shaping, and limitations as an unvalidated inspection. Reviewed archive cases add protocol captures, rubric scores, and human validation.

Fresh independent sessions matter for archive measurement. Targeted prompts are not same-session follow-ups to open prompts. Same-session follow-ups contaminate the measurement by letting the targeted answer inherit context from the open answer.

The goal is not a dramatic claim. The goal is a record another person can inspect.

From live inspection to public record

Today, Imbas has a Workbench, a Reader, and a public archive. The Workbench inspects the answer in front of you. The archive preserves reviewed cases that show recurring answer patterns across models and topics.
