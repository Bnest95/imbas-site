Concept

The Volunteer Gap

What the model knows.

What it surfaces.

What it leaves out.

That difference is the Volunteer Gap.

The Volunteer Gap measures the distance between what a model volunteers in an open answer and what it surfaces when asked directly.

Example pattern

Open question

The model gives a broad answer about a topic — common explanations, current debate, general context.

Direct question

The model surfaces a specific mechanism, regulation, date, or named source it did not mention in the open answer.

Gap

The mechanism was available to the model, but not volunteered. That measurable distance is the Volunteer Gap.

Signal patterns

  • Omission — relevant information absent from the open answer.
  • Framing Drift — the open answer emphasizes a different frame than direct prompting reveals.
  • Deflection — the open answer redirects rather than addresses the underlying concern.
